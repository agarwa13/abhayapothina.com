Amazon: UX Internship


I interned with Amazon.com from June 2014-Sept 2014 as a User Experience Designer. I was part of the Amazon Web Servicesâ€™ Training and Certification team.

I worked on designing a user interface for the information retrieval section of a new Learning Management System for the customers of AWS*. The information retrieval section is the entry point for users seeking training courses and certification tracks in the various AWS technologies.

My piece of the project consisted of conceptualizing and designing an interface for users to successfully browse and search for courses, and classroom classes available all over the globe.

UX Design Process

Phase 1

During the initial weeks of the project, I used the following methods to gather requirements and to plan.

Review Existing documentation
Competitive Analysis
Quantitative data collected from Adobe Omniture
Stakeholder Interviews (Infrastructure team, Marketing team, Curriculum team)

Outcome

Defined Design Objective
Identified existing and potential users
Project timeline

Phase 2

During the second phase, I created user task flows, and mapped user journeys. I also identified the essential filters for class search based on the data collected during phase 1.

I ideated over multiple ideas with weekly reviews with the stakeholders.

Phase 3

During the last phase, the final design was picked to be a path based training course display, where the user would see relevant training courses and certification based on his/her selection of role or curriculum level. This concept allowed to accommodate new paths apart from the just role and curriculum level, which was one of the design goals.
